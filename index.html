London
Stay: Premier Inn London Southwark (Tate Modern)  ·  Getting around: Tube & Elizabeth Line
5Nights
Day 1 6 Sep Saturday
Arrival & South Bank
DaytimeJenifer arrives Heathrow on the long-haul flight from Manila; Simon arrives from Belfast International. Both take the Elizabeth Line directly into central London — simplest connection, no changes. Check in, drop bags, get settled.
EveningShort South Bank walk to stretch the legs after the flight — Tate Modern exterior, Millennium Bridge, the riverfront. Dinner at a riverside restaurant. Early night; it is a very long travel day for Jenifer.
Tate Modern — freeElizabeth Line from LHR: ~£14pp
Day 2 7 Sep Sunday
Westminster & the West End
MorningWestminster walk. Start at Westminster Bridge for the Parliament and Big Ben view. St James’s Park — pelicans, deck chairs, the palace at the far end. Buckingham Palace exterior, Horse Guards Parade. No rush; this is a Sunday morning.
LunchHead north through Soho — Wardour Street or Greek Street for a relaxed lunch.
AfternoonNational Gallery on Trafalgar Square (free, open Sundays, 1.5–2hrs). Choose one or two rooms to explore properly. Trafalgar Square itself, then wander into Covent Garden for the afternoon.
EveningDinner in Covent Garden or Seven Dials — a wide range of good restaurants at every price point.
National Gallery — free, open SundaysAll walkable from Westminster Tube
Day 3 8 Sep Monday
East London
MorningTower Bridge & the Thames. Walk from London Bridge station. Tower Bridge exterior and the riverfront east past St Katharine Docks. Good views back across to the City skyline.
LunchBrick Lane. A salt beef bagel from Beigel Bake at the north end of the street — open 24hrs, a genuine London institution. Or one of the many cafes along the road.
AfternoonShoreditch & Spitalfields. Street art around Hanbury Street and Princelet Street. Spitalfields Market for a browse. This area rewards slow walking with no fixed plan.
EveningDinner in Shoreditch — one of London’s best areas for independent restaurants.
Beigel Bake — north end of Brick Lane, open 24hrsOverground from Shoreditch High St
Day 4 9 Sep Tuesday
Bloomsbury & Borough Market
MorningBritish Museum (free — arrive at opening, 10am). The Great Court is remarkable before the crowds arrive. Choose one or two wings to focus on rather than rushing everywhere. Allow 2.5hrs minimum.
LunchWalk south across the river to Borough Market for lunch. Open Tuesdays — a wide range of stalls. This is the meal of the day; take time with it.
AfternoonRelaxed South Bank walk east from Borough — Tate Modern galleries (free), Millennium Bridge, Shakespeare’s Globe exterior. A good hour’s wander with no agenda.
EveningDinner in Bermondsey or back in Soho. Early start is not needed tomorrow — the Windsor trip works best mid-morning.
British Museum — freeTate Modern — freeBorough Market: busiest Thu–Sat, good on Tue
Day 5 10 Sep Wednesday
Windsor Castle Day Trip
MorningTake the Elizabeth Line from Paddington to Windsor & Eton Central — about 40 minutes direct. Windsor Castle is the oldest and largest occupied castle in the world — home to the Royal Family for over 900 years. Pre-book timed entry. St George’s Chapel inside the castle grounds is one of the finest examples of Gothic architecture in England. Allow 2.5–3hrs for the castle.
LunchLunch in Windsor town — the streets around the castle have a good range of cafes and restaurants. The town is small and very easy to walk.
AfternoonWalk along the Long Walk — a three-mile avenue of trees stretching from the castle gates into Windsor Great Park. You do not need to walk the whole thing; even 20–30 minutes along it gives a sense of the extraordinary scale. Or explore the town itself before heading back.
EveningTrain back to London (~40 min). Quiet evening and pack for the move north to York tomorrow.
Pre-book Windsor Castle timed entryElizabeth Line to Windsor: ~40min from PaddingtonLong Walk & Windsor Great Park — free

Day 6 11 Sep Thursday
Arrive York — Medieval Streets
MorningCheck out of the London hotel. Tube to Kings Cross and board the LNER north. ~2hrs to York — a comfortable journey. Arrive early afternoon.
AfternoonCheck in at the hotel (5-min walk from the station). Head straight to The Shambles — the most famous medieval street in England, so narrow in places the overhanging buildings nearly touch. No ticket, no queue. Walk the surrounding lanes: Whip-Ma-Whop-Ma-Gate, the Shambles Market, the snickleways (York’s network of tiny alleyways).
Late PMWalk the City Walls — a free 3km circuit of the original Roman and medieval walls around the city. The stretch from Bootham Bar to Monk Bar has the best views over the Minster. Allow 1–1.5hrs.
EveningDinner in York. Gillygate and Micklegate have a good range of independent restaurants.
The Shambles — freeCity Walls walk — freeBook advance train: £20–40pp
Day 7 12 Sep Friday
York Minster, Museums & Betty’s
MorningYork Minster. Pre-book the tower climb for a panoramic view over the whole city — it is the best vantage point in York. The cathedral itself is one of the finest Gothic buildings in Northern Europe; allow 1.5hrs inside without hurrying.
Late AMNational Railway Museum (free, a 10-min walk from the Minster). One of the genuinely great free museums in Britain — the Flying Scotsman, a Japanese bullet train, the royal carriages. Allow 1.5hrs; it is more engaging than it sounds.
LunchBetty’s Tea Room on St Helen’s Square. Expect a queue of 15–20 minutes — it moves steadily and is worth every minute. A proper Yorkshire institution that has been in the same building since 1936.
AfternoonRelaxed afternoon in the city. Clifford’s Tower (the remains of York’s Norman castle, with views over the city) is worth the short climb. Or a slow walk along the River Ouse and the riverside gardens.
EveningFinal York dinner. Early night — the train to Edinburgh leaves tomorrow morning.
National Railway Museum — freePre-book York Minster tower climbBetty’s: short queue, absolutely worth it

4Nights
Day 8 13 Sep Saturday
Arrive Edinburgh — First Evening on the Royal Mile
MorningTrain from York. ~2hrs 30min, arriving Edinburgh Waverley early-to-mid afternoon. The hotel is a 10-minute walk up the Royal Mile from the station.
AfternoonCheck in, drop bags. First walk along the Royal Mile — explore the closes (the narrow alleyways branching off either side). Each one leads somewhere unexpected: a hidden courtyard, a staircase, a view. No plan needed.
EveningDinner in the Grassmarket — a lively square at the foot of the castle rock, surrounded by restaurants. A good introduction to the Old Town atmosphere.
York → Edinburgh Waverley: ~2hrs 30minHotel: 10-min walk from Waverley
Day 9 14 Sep Sunday
Edinburgh Castle & Old Town
MorningEdinburgh Castle. Pre-book a timed entry and arrive at opening. The Crown Jewels, the Stone of Destiny, the One O’Clock Gun. The views from the castle ramparts over the city, Arthur’s Seat, and out to the Firth of Forth are extraordinary. Allow 2.5–3hrs — there is considerably more to see here than most people expect.
LunchDescend the Royal Mile for lunch at one of the older pubs just off the Mile — The Jolly Judge (down a close near the castle) is a quiet, unpretentious option away from the main tourist flow.
AfternoonOld Town wander. St Giles’ Cathedral (free — the Thistle Chapel inside is extraordinary). Victoria Street — the curved, colourful street said to have inspired Diagon Alley. Greyfriars Kirkyard and the Greyfriars Bobby statue. This is a neighbourhood to explore slowly rather than tick off.
EveningCalton Hill at sunset. A 5-minute walk from the east end of Princes Street — a short climb to a hilltop with 360° views over the entire city. One of Edinburgh’s best kept free secrets. The light on a clear September evening is remarkable.
Pre-book Edinburgh Castle: sells out in SeptemberSt Giles’ Cathedral — freeGreyfriars Kirkyard — freeCalton Hill — free
Day 10 15 Sep Monday
Arthur’s Seat & New Town
MorningArthur’s Seat. Start early (8–9am) before the day heats up. The main path from Holyrood is clearly marked and not technically difficult — about 45 minutes to the summit at a comfortable pace. The views from the top over Edinburgh, the Firth of Forth, and the Pentland Hills are extraordinary on a clear September morning. Bring a layer; it is always windy at the top.
Late AMWalk back down past Holyrood Palace exterior. Coffee and a sit-down at a café at the foot of the Royal Mile — well earned after the hike.
LunchScottish National Gallery (free, on the Mound between Old and New Town). A manageable highlights visit of 45–60 minutes. Lunch at the gallery café or on Princes Street.
AfternoonNew Town. Princes Street Gardens — the view up to the castle from here is one of the best in the city. George Street for a browse. The Georgian New Town is a complete contrast to the medieval Old Town; quieter and more elegant.
EveningDinner in the New Town — George Street and Thistle Street have a strong selection of independent restaurants.
Arthur’s Seat — freeNational Gallery — freeGood walking shoes for the hike
Day 11 16 Sep Tuesday
Leith & the Royal Yacht Britannia
MorningBus 16 or the tram from Princes Street down to Leith — about 20 minutes. Royal Yacht Britannia (pre-book; allow 2hrs). The royal family’s private floating home from 1954 to 1997, preserved exactly as it was. One of the most quietly fascinating things to visit in Scotland — the scale of it, the attention to detail in every cabin, the contrast between the formal state rooms and the tiny crew quarters.
LunchLunch along The Shore — Leith’s old waterfront street, lined with good independent restaurants and cafes with outdoor seating. A completely different feel from the tourist centre of Edinburgh.
AfternoonBack to the city. National Museum of Scotland (free, on Chambers Street near the Royal Mile — one of the best free museums in the UK). Covers everything from ancient Scotland to modern design. Allow 1.5–2hrs and just follow what interests you.
EveningFinal Edinburgh dinner. Pack bags tonight — flying to Belfast tomorrow.
Pre-book Royal Yacht BritanniaNational Museum of Scotland — freeBus 16 from Princes St to Leith: ~20min

Day 12 17 Sep Wednesday
Arrive Belfast — Cathedral Quarter
MorningMorning free in Edinburgh before check-out. Tram from St Andrew Square to the airport (~30min). Fly Edinburgh to Belfast (~1hr). Simon collects the car from BFS long-stay parking. Short drive into the city centre.
AfternoonCheck in. Walk the Cathedral Quarter — the oldest part of the city, centred around St Anne’s Cathedral. The narrow entries (Belfast’s historic alleyways) off High Street and Donegall Street are worth exploring slowly. A very different character to anything seen so far on the trip.
EveningSt Anne’s Cathedral (free, worth going inside — the interior is striking, particularly the mosaic ceiling). Dinner in the Cathedral Quarter — Hill Street and Exchange Street have a good selection.
St Anne’s Cathedral — freeEDI → BFS: ~1hr flight
Day 13 18 Sep Thursday
Giant’s Causeway Day Trip
MorningDrive north from Belfast on the A2 coastal road — about 1hr 30min to the Giant’s Causeway. Pre-book a timed entry and arrive early before the day-tripper coaches. A UNESCO World Heritage Site: 40,000 interlocking basalt columns formed by volcanic activity 60 million years ago, stepping down into the North Atlantic. Completely unlike anything else in the British Isles.
MiddayWalk the coastal path both above and below the columns — the cliff-top trail gives a completely different perspective. Allow 2–2.5hrs on site. The signage is good; just follow the coastal path at your own pace.
LunchLunch at the Causeway visitor centre café, or a short drive to the village of Ballintoy Harbour for something quieter and more scenic.
AfternoonDrive back along the Causeway Coastal Route — the cliff scenery on the return through Cushendall and Larne is outstanding. Stop whenever something catches your eye. Back in Belfast by early evening.
EveningEasy dinner back in the city — a quieter evening after a full day outdoors.
Pre-book Giant’s Causeway timed entryDrive from Belfast: ~1hr 30min each wayFull day — start by 9am
Day 14 19 Sep Friday
St George’s Market, Titanic & The Murals
8amSt George’s Market opens (Friday is the Food & Garden Market — the best day to visit). A beautiful Victorian covered market with street food, local produce, arts and crafts. Widely regarded as the best market in Ireland. A proper breakfast from a stall, a browse, no rush. Closes at 2pm but go early for the full atmosphere.
10amTitanic Belfast. Pre-book the 10am slot. A 10-minute drive or taxi from the market. Allow 3 hours — this is genuinely one of the best museums in Europe. The building itself (shaped like the prows of four ships) is remarkable before you even go inside. The exhibition covers the design, construction, launch, sinking, and legacy of the Titanic in full.
LunchLunch in the Titanic Quarter — the Titanic Hotel’s bar and restaurant is a good option in a beautifully restored Edwardian building.
AfternoonBlack taxi political murals tour. Book through one of the well-reviewed local taxi companies. ~1.5hrs covering the Falls Road and Shankill Road murals and the Peace Wall. Essential Belfast — hearing the story of the Troubles from a local who lived through it is unlike any museum or documentary. Nothing else on the trip will stay with you quite like this.
EveningFarewell dinner — book somewhere worth the occasion in the Cathedral Quarter.
St George’s Market: Fri 8am–2pm — arrive earlyPre-book Titanic Belfast: 10am slotBook black taxi murals tour in advance

Visa note — UK Standard Visitor Visa & Short Stay Visa Waiver Programme (SSVWP)
Jenifer will need a UK Standard Visitor Visa before travelling. This itinerary works as a key supporting document: it shows a clear route with exact dates (6–20 September 2026), named hotels, realistic travel connections, and a confirmed return flight on 20 September. The most important supporting documents are Jenifer’s IBM employment letter (confirming role, salary, and approved leave), 3 months of bank statements, and Simon’s invitation letter as the UK-based contact.

As a Philippine national, Jenifer qualifies for Ireland’s Short Stay Visa Waiver Programme (SSVWP). This means a valid UK Standard Visitor Visa also permits entry to the Republic of Ireland for up to 90 days — no separate Irish visa is required. The SSVWP is open to nationals of the Philippines (among other countries) and is distinct from the British-Irish Visa Scheme (BIVS), which applies only to Chinese and Indian nationals and does not apply here.
