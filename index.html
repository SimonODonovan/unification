Day 1 6 Sep Saturday
Arrival & South Bank
DaytimeJenifer arrives Heathrow on a long-haul flight from Manila; Simon arrives from Belfast International. Both take the Elizabeth Line directly into central London — easiest connection, no changes needed. Check in, drop bags, get oriented.
EveningShort South Bank walk to stretch the legs — Tate Modern exterior, Millennium Bridge, Southbank Centre. Dinner at a pub along the river. Early night; it is a very long travel day for Jenifer.

Day 2 7 Sep Sunday
Westminster & the West End
MorningWestminster walk. Start at Westminster Bridge for the Parliament and Big Ben view. St James’s Park — pelicans, deck chairs, the palace at the far end. Buckingham Palace exterior, Horse Guards Parade. No rush.
LunchHead north into Soho — Wardour Street or Greek Street for a relaxed pub lunch.
AfternoonNational Gallery on Trafalgar Square (free, 1.5–2hrs). Pick one or two rooms rather than trying to cover everything. Trafalgar Square, then wander down to Covent Garden.
EveningDinner in Covent Garden or Seven Dials — good range of restaurants at every price point.
National Gallery — free & open SundaysAll walkable from Westminster Tube
Day 3 8 Sep Monday
East London
MorningTower Bridge area. Walk from London Bridge station. Tower Bridge exterior, the riverfront walk east past St Katharine Docks. Good views back across to the City.
LunchBrick Lane — a salt beef bagel from Beigel Bake (open 24hrs, a true London institution at the north end of the street) or lunch at one of the many cafes along the road.
AfternoonShoreditch & Spitalfields. Street art around Hanbury Street and Princelet Street. Spitalfields Market for a browse. This area rewards slow walking with no fixed agenda.
EveningDinner in Shoreditch — one of the best areas in London for independent restaurants. Or get the Overground back to Soho.
Beigel Bake — open 24hrs, north end of Brick LaneOverground from Shoreditch High St
Day 4 9 Sep Tuesday
Bloomsbury & Borough Market
MorningBritish Museum (free — arrive at opening, 10am). The Great Court is remarkable before the crowds arrive. Choose one or two wings to explore properly rather than rushing through everything. Allow 2.5hrs.
LunchWalk south across the river to Borough Market. Open Tuesday — good range of stalls for lunch. This is the meal of the day; take your time with it.
AfternoonRelaxed South Bank walk east from Borough — Tate Modern galleries (free), Millennium Bridge, Shakespeare’s Globe exterior. A good hour’s wander with no agenda.
EveningDinner in Borough or Bermondsey area, or cross back into Soho. Early start tomorrow: train to York.
British Museum — freeTate Modern — freeBorough Market: busiest Thu–Sat but good on Tue
Day 5 10 Sep Wednesday
Slow Morning — Train to York
MorningRelaxed last London morning. Check out, store bags at the hotel, get a coffee. No sightseeing agenda — just a gentle final few hours in the city.
MiddayCollect bags and take the Tube to Kings Cross. LNER train north to York — ~2hrs. The 11am–1pm trains work well. Sit back and watch England roll past.
AfternoonArrive York 1–3pm. Check in. Walk to the Shambles — the famous medieval street. No ticket, no queue, just a wander. Explore the lanes off it.
EveningDinner in York — Gillygate and Micklegate have a good range of independent restaurants. The city is compact and very pleasant to walk after dark.
Book advance train: £20–40ppKings Cross → York: ~2hrs

Day 6 11 Sep Thursday
York Morning — Train to Edinburgh
MorningYork Minster. Pre-book the tower climb for panoramic views over the city. The cathedral itself is extraordinary — one of the finest Gothic buildings in Northern Europe. Allow 1.5hrs inside without rushing.
Late AMNational Railway Museum (free). Genuinely one of the best free museums in Britain — the Flying Scotsman, a Japanese bullet train, royal carriages. Allow 1.5hrs. Sit on the benches and just look up.
LunchBetty’s Tea Room on St Helen’s Square. Expect a 15–20 minute queue outside — it moves steadily and it is absolutely worth it. A proper Yorkshire institution.
AfternoonTrain to Edinburgh Waverley — ~2hrs 30min. The Northumberland coast stretch has sea views; sit on the right-hand (east) side of the train. Arrive Edinburgh ~5–6pm.
EveningCheck in to the Royal Mile hotel. Short walk to explore a close or two. Dinner in the Grassmarket — a lively square at the foot of the castle rock.
National Railway Museum — freePre-book York Minster tower climbBetty’s: short queue, worth every minuteYork → Edinburgh: ~2hrs 30min

Edinburgh

4Nights
Day 7 12 Sep Friday
Old Town & the Castle
MorningEdinburgh Castle. Pre-book a timed entry and arrive at opening. The Crown Jewels, the Stone of Destiny, the One O’Clock Gun at 1pm. The views from the ramparts over the city and out to the Firth of Forth are remarkable. Allow 2.5–3hrs — there is more here than most people expect.
LunchDescend the Royal Mile. Pub lunch at one of the older pubs just off the Mile — The Jolly Judge (down a close near the castle) is a good quiet option away from the main tourist flow.
AfternoonOld Town wander. St Giles’ Cathedral (free — beautiful interior, the Thistle Chapel is extraordinary). Victoria Street — the curved, colourful street said to have inspired Diagon Alley. Greyfriars Kirkyard. The Grassmarket. This is a neighbourhood to explore slowly, not a checklist.
EveningDinner in the Old Town. Grassmarket has a good range — Maxie’s Bistro has outdoor seating on the square when the weather allows.
Pre-book Edinburgh Castle: sells out in SepSt Giles’ Cathedral — freeGreyfriars Kirkyard — free
Day 8 13 Sep Saturday
Arthur’s Seat & New Town
MorningArthur’s Seat. Start early (8–9am) before the day gets busy — especially important on a Saturday. The main path from Holyrood is well-marked and straightforward. About 1.5hrs up and back. The summit views over the city, the Firth of Forth, and the Pentlands are extraordinary on a clear September morning. Bring a layer; it is always windy at the top.
Late AMWalk back past Holyrood Palace exterior. Coffee and a sit-down at a café nearby — you have earned it after the hike.
LunchScottish National Gallery (free, 1.5hrs). Walk up through the Mound — the gallery sits at the junction of Old and New Town with fine views. Lunch at the gallery café or on Princes Street.
AfternoonNew Town. Princes Street Gardens (the view up to the castle from here is one of the best in the city). George Street, Rose Street. Browse and decompress — the Georgian New Town is a strong contrast to the medieval Old Town.
EveningThe Bow Bar on Victoria Street — one of the finest selections of Scottish malt whisky in Edinburgh, no music, no gimmicks. Just good whisky and conversation.
Arthur’s Seat — freeNational Gallery — freeWear walking shoes for the hikeStart early on Saturdays to avoid crowds
Day 9 14 Sep Sunday
Leith & the Royal Yacht Britannia
MorningBus 16 or tram from Princes Street down to Leith — about 20 minutes. The Royal Yacht Britannia is one of the most fascinating things to visit in Scotland (pre-book; allow 2hrs). The royal family’s private floating home from 1954 to 1997 — every room is preserved exactly as it was.
LunchLunch in Leith. The area around The Shore — Leith’s old waterfront street — has several excellent restaurants and cafés, a different and more local feel than the Old Town.
AfternoonRelaxed afternoon back in the city. Calton Hill is a short walk from the east end of Princes Street — five minutes to climb, excellent 360° views over the city. An easy and rewarding end to the Edinburgh days.
EveningFinal Edinburgh dinner. The area around the Royal Mile has plenty of options — try something with Scottish ingredients if you have not already.
Pre-book Royal Yacht Britannia (open Sundays)Calton Hill — free, 5-min climbBus 16 from Princes St to Leith

LunchQuick lunch near the Royal Mile. Check out and collect bags.
AfternoonTram from St Andrew Square direct to Edinburgh Airport — ~30min. Fly Edinburgh → Belfast International. ~1hr flight. Simon collects the car from BFS long-stay (parked since Day 1). Short drive into Belfast city centre.

Day 11 16 Sep Tuesday
Titanic Belfast
MorningTitanic Belfast. Pre-book the first entry slot (9am). Drive or take a short taxi from the hotel — about 10 minutes. Allow 3 full hours. This is genuinely one of the best museums in Europe, not just a Belfast attraction. The building itself — shaped like the prow of four ships — is extraordinary before you even go inside.
LunchLunch near the Titanic Quarter. The Titanic Hotel bar is a relaxed option in a beautiful Edwardian building. Or drive back into the city centre.
AfternoonRelaxed afternoon. Walk along the River Lagan, or explore the Cathedral Quarter at a quieter pace. Good coffee shops and independent bookshops around Commercial Court.
EveningCrown Liquor Saloon on Great Victoria Street — a Victorian gin palace owned by the National Trust, unlike anything else in Britain or Ireland. One drink here is not optional. Dinner nearby on the same street.
Pre-book Titanic Belfast: book the 9am slotCrown Liquor Saloon — National Trust
Day 12 17 Sep Wednesday
The Murals & Cathedral Quarter
MorningBlack taxi political murals tour. Book through one of the well-reviewed local taxi companies. About 1.5hrs covering the Falls Road and Shankill Road murals — both sides of the peace wall. Essential Belfast: a moving and genuinely illuminating experience of the city’s recent history. Nothing quite prepares you for it, and hearing it from a local who lived through it is unlike any museum.
LunchBack into the Cathedral Quarter for lunch. Good independent cafés around Dunbar Link and Exchange Street.
AfternoonSt Anne’s Cathedral (free — striking modern interior with a stainless steel spire). Walk the Cathedral Quarter lanes — Commercial Court, the narrow entries off High Street. This part of Belfast has a very different character to the Titanic Quarter; it is old, compact, and full of history.
EveningDinner in the Cathedral Quarter. The area around Hill Street and Exchange Street has a good mix of restaurants.
Book black taxi murals tour in advanceSt Anne’s Cathedral — free
Day 13 18 Sep Thursday
Giant’s Causeway Day Trip
MorningDrive north from Belfast on the A2 coastal road — about 1hr 30min to the Giant’s Causeway. A UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most spectacular natural landscapes in the British Isles: 40,000 interlocking basalt columns formed by ancient volcanic activity, stepping down into the North Atlantic. Arrive early to beat the tour groups.
MiddayWalk the coastal path above and below the columns. The cliffside walk back up to the visitor centre gives the best views. Allow 2–2.5hrs on the causeway itself.
LunchLunch at the Giant’s Causeway Visitor Centre café, or drive a few minutes to the village of Bushmills — a proper lunch pub and home to the oldest licensed whiskey distillery in the world (Old Bushmills, established 1608).
AfternoonOptional: a short drive along the Causeway Coastal Route on the return — the views of the Antrim coast, the Carrick-a-Rede rope bridge area, and the cliffs are outstanding. Or drive straight back to Belfast (~1hr 30min). Either way, a full and beautiful day.
EveningEasy dinner back in Belfast — a quieter evening after a full day outdoors.
Pre-book Giant’s Causeway timed entry (NTS)Drive from Belfast: ~1hr 30min each wayBushmills Distillery — world’s oldest
Day 14 19 Sep Friday
St George’s Market & Last Day in Belfast
MorningSt George’s Market — Friday is the “Food & Garden Market” (opens 8am, closes 2pm). A beautiful Victorian covered market with street food, local produce, arts and crafts. Widely regarded as the best market in Ireland. Perfect for a leisurely last morning, a proper breakfast from a stall, and a browse.
LunchEat at the market, or walk the short distance to the city centre for a sit-down lunch.
AfternoonFree afternoon — no agenda. Belfast city centre is compact and walkable. Good for a last slow wander, any final shopping on Royal Avenue, or simply sitting in a café. This is the last full day together on this trip.
EveningFarewell dinner. The Cathedral Quarter has the best choice of restaurants — book in advance for somewhere worth the occasion.
St George’s Market: Fri 8am–2pm — arrive earlyLast full day together
