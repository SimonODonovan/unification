Day 1 ✈️
Arrival & Recovery
DaytimeJenifer arrives Heathrow; Simon arrives from Belfast International. Both take the Elizabeth Line directly into central London — easiest, no changes. Check in, drop bags, get oriented.
EveningSouth Bank walk. Short stroll along the riverside — Tate Modern exterior, Millennium Bridge, Southbank Centre. Dinner at a riverside pub. Early night; it's a long travel day for Jenifer.
Tate Modern — free Elizabeth Line from LHR ~£14pp
Day 2 🏛️
Westminster & the West End
MorningWestminster walk. Start at Westminster Bridge for the classic Parliament + Big Ben view. St James’s Park (worth lingering — pelicans, deck chairs, the palace at the far end). Buckingham Palace exterior, Horse Guards Parade.
LunchHead north into Soho — Berwick Street Market area or a pub on Wardour Street.
AfternoonNational Gallery on Trafalgar Square (free, 1.5–2hrs). Don’t try to see everything — pick one wing. Trafalgar Square itself, then wander down to Covent Garden.
EveningDinner in Covent Garden or Seven Dials area — good mix of restaurants at all price points.
National Gallery — free All walkable from Westminster Tube
Day 3 📚
Bloomsbury & Borough
MorningBritish Museum (free, aim to arrive at opening — 10am). The Great Court is remarkable before the crowds arrive. Pick one or two collections to focus on rather than rushing everywhere. Allow 2.5hrs.
LunchWalk south across the river to Borough Market for lunch — one of the best food markets in Europe. No need to rush; this is the meal of the day.
AfternoonRelaxed riverside afternoon. Walk the South Bank east — Tate Modern galleries (free), Shakespeare’s Globe exterior, the riverbank path. This is a good hour’s wander with no agenda.
EveningDinner in Bermondsey or Borough — strong independent restaurant scene here. Or cross back to Soho.
British Museum — free Tate Modern — free Borough Market: go hungry
Day 4 🧱
East London
MorningTower Bridge & the Thames. Walk from London Bridge station. Tower Bridge exterior, area around the Tower of London (exterior — optional to go inside, 2–3hrs if so). Walk east along the river.
LunchBrick Lane — salt beef bagel at Beigel Bake (open 24hrs, a true London institution) or one of the many cafes along the street.
AfternoonShoreditch & Spitalfields. Street art around Hanbury Street and Princelet Street. Spitalfields Market for a browse. No agenda — this area rewards slow walking.
EveningDinner in Shoreditch — one of the best areas in London for food. Or get the Overground back to Soho for a last London night out. Pack for York.
Overground / District Line Beigel Bake — open 24hrs
Day 5 🚆
Slow Morning — Train North to York
MorningRelaxed last morning in London. Coffee, a walk, no sightseeing agenda. Good opportunity for any last errand or just sitting in a café.
MiddayLNER train from Kings Cross to York. ~2hrs. Book in advance — the 11am–1pm window gives a comfortable arrival.
AfternoonArrive York ~1–3pm. Check in. Walk the city centre — York is compact and extremely walkable. The Shambles at your own pace; no queue, no ticket, just a medieval street.
EveningDinner in York — the city has a good range of independent restaurants around Gillygate and Micklegate.
Kings Cross → York: ~2hrs Book advance trains: £20–40pp

Day 6 ⛪
York — Then North to Edinburgh
MorningYork Minster. Pre-book the tower climb for panoramic views over the city (worth it). The cathedral itself is extraordinary — allow 1.5hrs inside. Don’t rush it.
Late AMNational Railway Museum (free — genuinely one of the best free museums in Britain). Flying Scotsman, the Japanese bullet train, royal carriages. Allow 1.5hrs.
LunchBack into the city centre. Betty’s Tea Room if you’re happy to queue (usually 15–20 min — absolutely worth it). Or a pub lunch on Stonegate.
AfternoonTrain to Edinburgh. York to Edinburgh Waverley ~2hrs 30min — the Northumberland coast stretch is one of the great British rail journeys. Arrive ~4–6pm depending on your train.
EveningCheck in. Walk to the Royal Mile — a 5–10 minute stroll from Waverley. Explore a close or two. Dinner in the Grassmarket.
National Railway Museum — free Pre-book York Minster tower Betty’s: expect a short queue

Day 7 🏰
Old Town & the Castle
MorningEdinburgh Castle. Pre-book a timed entry and arrive at opening. The Crown Jewels, the Stone of Destiny, the One O’Clock Gun (if you time it right). The views from the ramparts alone justify the ticket. Allow 2.5–3hrs; there’s more here than most people expect.
LunchDescend the Royal Mile. Pub lunch at one of the older pubs off the Mile — The Jolly Judge (down a close) is a good, quiet option.
AfternoonOld Town wander. St Giles’ Cathedral (free, beautiful inside). Victoria Street — the curved, colourful street said to have inspired Diagon Alley. Greyfriars Kirkyard. Grassmarket browse. This is not a list to complete; it’s a neighbourhood to explore.
EveningDinner in the Old Town. The Grassmarket has a good range of options — Maxie’s Bistro is a reliable mid-range choice with outdoor seating.
Pre-book Edinburgh Castle: sells out St Giles’ Cathedral — free Greyfriars Kirkyard — free
Day 8 ⛰️
Arthur’s Seat & New Town
MorningArthur’s Seat. Start early (8–9am) before the day gets busy. The main path from Holyrood is straightforward — about 1.5hrs up and back. The summit views over Edinburgh, the Firth of Forth, and the Pentlands are extraordinary on a clear day. Bring layers — it’s always windy at the top.
Late AMWalk back via Holyrood Palace exterior. Coffee and a sit-down near the park — you’ve earned it.
LunchWalk up through the Old Town to the Scottish National Gallery (free, 30–45 min is enough for a highlights visit). Lunch at the gallery café or nearby on the Mound.
AfternoonNew Town. Princes Street Gardens (the view up to the castle from here is one of the best in the city). George Street, Rose Street. Browse and decompress — this is the lower-key, Georgian half of Edinburgh and a good contrast to the Old Town.
EveningWhisky bar or classic Edinburgh pub. The Bow Bar on Victoria Street has one of the finest selections of Scotch malt whisky in the city, and no music — just good whisky and conversation.
Arthur’s Seat — free National Gallery — free Wear good walking shoes
Day 9 ⚓
Leith & Depart for Belfast
MorningLeith. Take bus 16 or the tram from Princes Street — about 20 minutes. The Royal Yacht Britannia is excellent (pre-book; allow 2hrs). The royal family’s private floating home — fascinating and completely different from anything else on the trip. The Leith waterfront is also a good stroll.
LunchLunch in Leith — the area around The Shore has several good restaurants and cafés with waterfront seating.
AfternoonBus back to city. Check out, collect bags. Head to Edinburgh Airport by tram (direct from city centre, ~30 min). Fly Edinburgh → Belfast International. ~1hr flight.
EveningArrive BFS. Simon collects the car from long-stay parking (car has been here since Day 1). Short drive into Belfast city centre. Check in. Easy first evening — Cathedral Quarter walk, a drink at the Duke of York bar.
Pre-book Royal Yacht Britannia Tram: Princes St → Airport ~30min EDI → BFS: Loganair / easyJet ~£50–80pp

Day 10 🚢
Titanic Belfast
MorningTitanic Belfast. Pre-book the first entry slot (9am). Drive or take a short taxi from the hotel. Allow 3 full hours — this is genuinely one of the best museums in Europe, not just a tourist attraction. The building itself is extraordinary.
LunchLunch near the Titanic Quarter — the Titanic Hotel bar is a relaxed option. Or drive back into the city centre.
AfternoonBlack taxi political murals tour. Book through one of the well-reviewed local taxi companies. ~1.5hrs, covering the Falls Road and Shankill Road murals. Essential Belfast — a moving and genuinely illuminating experience of the city’s recent history. Nothing else prepares you for it quite like hearing it from a local.
EveningCrown Liquor Saloon — a Victorian gin palace on Great Victoria Street, owned by the National Trust. One drink here is mandatory. Dinner nearby on the same street or Botanic Avenue.
Pre-book Titanic Belfast: often sells out Book black taxi tour in advance Crown Liquor Saloon — National Trust pub
Day 11 🏙️
Cathedral Quarter & Final Day
MorningCathedral Quarter. St Anne’s Cathedral (free, striking interior). The narrow lanes around the Duke of York — best explored on foot with no agenda. St George’s Market if visiting on a Friday, Saturday, or Sunday — one of the best covered markets in Ireland, with street food, local produce, and crafts.
LunchLunch in the Cathedral Quarter area. Good coffee and casual food at the many independent spots around Hill Street and Exchange Street.
AfternoonFree afternoon. Belfast has a compact city centre — good for a last wander, shopping on Royal Avenue, or simply sitting in a café with no plan. From here, continue to Ireland or depart. The car is available for onward driving south across the open border — no checks, no stop.
St Anne’s Cathedral — free St George’s Market: Fri, Sat & Sun only Border crossing south at Newry: open road
